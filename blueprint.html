Section 1

Company Context

Founder: Solo. Two B2B SaaS products at $600K and $700K ARR respectively. Total combined ARR: $1.3M+. No employees. Both products run simultaneously with the founder holding all threads.

The constraint, in the founder's own words:

"I can't do outbound at all. There aren't enough hours. Between managing support for two products and actually building features, there's nothing left for proactive sales."

This is the real problem: not that outbound is hard, but that it requires human hours the founder doesn't have. Every task routes through the same single point. That's the ceiling. The AI company changes the architecture, not just the task list.

Section 2

Current State Audit

What's working — don't touch these

  • Product-market fit on both products. Customers are paying, churn is low.
  • Billing and subscriptions fully automated via Stripe.
  • Support ticketing installed (Intercom) but first responses are founder-written.

What's falling through the cracks

  • Outbound: Not running. All growth is organic/word-of-mouth. No systematic outreach to expansion accounts or new segments.
  • Support triage: Tier-1 tickets ("how do I do X?", billing questions) handled by founder — same person responsible for product decisions.
  • Pipeline visibility: No CRM. Pipeline lives in the founder's head.
  • Content: No consistent publishing cadence. Social posting is sporadic.

Biggest time drains (ranked by founder)

  1. First-response support across two Intercom inboxes
  2. Inbound sales questions without a defined process
  3. Anything marketing-related — either the founder does it, or it doesn't happen
Section 3

Your AI Company — Agents Activated

⚡

Sales Agent

Outbound, prospecting, pipeline management

Owns

  • ICP research and prospect list building for both products
  • Contact enrichment: company data, team size, tool stack from public sources
  • Personalized first-touch emails written in the founder's voice — reviewed before sending until trust is established
  • Follow-up sequence management: 3-touch automated, founder approves anything off-template
  • CRM updates: every prospect interaction logged automatically (HubSpot Free)
  • Weekly pipeline report: open conversations, response rates, stage breakdown

Does not do

  • Close deals — founder closes, or a defined close script handles standard-sized deals
  • Make pricing decisions without approval
  • Send anything outside approved templates without flagging first

Escalation triggers comes to you

  • Any deal above $500/month annual contract value
  • Any prospect asking a question outside the objection library
  • Any negative response requiring judgment (not a form reply)

Day 7 success criteria

  • 20 qualified prospects identified and loaded into CRM for both products
  • 10 personalized first-touch drafts ready for founder approval
  • Sequence templates live, tested, and firing
💬

Support Agent

Tier-1 triage, first response, knowledge base

Owns

  • Tier-1 ticket triage across both Intercom inboxes
  • First response on: how-to questions, feature clarification, billing inquiries, integration questions
  • Knowledge base updates: every ticket resolved by the agent that isn't in the KB gets added automatically
  • Escalation routing: Tier-2 tickets (bug reports, feature requests, refund decisions) flagged within 30 minutes
  • Response time target: under 2 hours for Tier-1, under 4 hours for escalation flag

Does not do

  • Issue refunds — founder approves
  • Respond to churn-risk signals without flagging immediately
  • Make product decisions disguised as support answers

Escalation triggers comes to you

  • Any refund request above $50
  • Any user reporting a potential security issue
  • Any conversation that goes negative (3+ messages, unresolved)
  • Any feature request from a high-value account

Day 7 success criteria

  • Average first response under 2 hours for Tier-1 tickets
  • Zero unread tickets older than 4 hours in Intercom
  • Founder has not personally responded to a single Tier-1 ticket

Agents not activated at pilot

Available in month 2+ once Sales and Support are stable:

  • Marketing Agent — content calendar, social distribution, email campaigns. Activated once escalation patterns from Sales/Support are understood.
  • Ops Agent — scheduling, vendor coordination, project tracking. Lower priority at solo stage; revisit before first hire.

Section 5

What Moves Off Your Plate

Starting Day 1 of the pilot, you stop doing these things:

Support

  • First response to every ticket
  • Answering "have you tried..." questions
  • Responding to billing questions
  • Re-reading old threads for context

Sales

  • Manual prospect research
  • Writing first-touch outreach
  • Managing the follow-up sequence
  • CRM entry and updates

You stay responsible for: Responding to escalations within 24 business hours. Approving new outreach templates. Product decisions. Closing high-value deals.
